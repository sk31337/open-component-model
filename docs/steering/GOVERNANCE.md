# TSC Governance

This document describes governance procedures specific to the OCM Technical Steering Committee (TSC).

For general community governance, roles, decision-making principles, ways of working, and specification development process, see the [OCM Specification Governance](https://github.com/open-component-model/ocm-spec/blob/main/5._Governance.md).

## Scope

The TSC is responsible for all technical oversight of the Open Component Model project as defined in the [Project Charter](./CHARTER.md).

## Membership

TSC voting members and the current Chair are listed in [OWNERS.md](./OWNERS.md).

New voting members may be added by majority approval of existing voting members. Removal of a voting member requires majority approval of the remaining voting members. The TSC may elect a Chair who presides over meetings and serves as the primary contact between the project and NeoNephos Foundation / LF Europe.

## Voting

- Quorum requires at least 50% of all voting members to be present.
- Decisions at a meeting require a majority vote of those in attendance, provided quorum is met.
- Decisions by electronic vote without a meeting require a majority of all voting members.
- Charter amendments and license exceptions require a two-thirds vote of the entire TSC.
- Deadlocked votes are escalated to the TAC.

For full voting procedures, see the [Project Charter, Section 3](./CHARTER.md).

## Meetings

The TSC holds monthly meetings. Meeting notes are published in [meeting-notes/](./meeting-notes/).

Meetings are intended to be open to the public and may be conducted electronically, via teleconference, or in person. Contact the Chair for an invitation (see [OWNERS.md](./OWNERS.md)).

## Relationship to Working Groups and SIGs

The TSC approves the creation, modification, and dissolution of Working Groups and SIGs. SIG charters must be submitted to the TSC for formal vote as described in the [SIG Handbook](../community/SIGs/SIG-Handbook.md).

Major SIG decisions — such as charter changes, leadership changes, or dissolution — require TSC approval in addition to the SIG's own supermajority vote.

## Security

The Security Officer role is currently delegated to the entire TSC. The TSC can be reached via [mail](mailto:open-component-model-tsc@lists.neonephos.org).
